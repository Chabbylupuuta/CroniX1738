Tonga Bull

Chief & CEO

About Me

The name "Tonga Bull" has been echoing through the halls of CBU for longer than anyone can remember. But who is this culinary legend? None other than Martin Mwinga, the CEO extraordinaire of the legendary Tonga Bull restaurant.

This reporter tracked him down in his natural habitat (aka The Kitchen) to unravel the mystery behind the man and his meaty masterpiece.

Talk about spice your life

From Bedroom Chef to Big-Time Boss

Turns out, Martin's always had a fire in his belly (both literally and figuratively). Cooking isn't just a job for him, it's a symphony of sizzling spices and a ballet of bubbling pots.

"Nshima with some fried or boiled fish? Child's play," he declares with a wink. "Lumanda?

Now that's where the magic happens." You can practically see the culinary passion radiating from his eyes like heat waves off a fresh batch of hot chips.

Learning from the Law (and the Stove)

Last year wasn't just about flipping chicken breasts for Martin.

It was a crash course in entrepreneurship (and maybe a little legal education).

Running a restaurant from his dorm room wasn't exactly kosher, and the authorities, bless their hearts, just wanted to ensure he was following the rules. "No hard feelings," says Martin, "they were just doing their job." But hey, even confiscated spatulas couldn't dampen his culinary spirit.

Chicken, Chips, and Customer Service that Could Melt a Glacier What keeps the students flocking to Tonga Bull like pigeons to a freshly dropped croissant? Well, for starters, the food is next-level. We're talking chicken and chips that would make Colonel Sanders weep tears of joy. But it's not just the grub that keeps them coming back.

The customer service is smoother than a vat of well-seasoned gravy. Got a complaint about your meal? The Tonga Bull crew will not only refund your money,

They'll throw in another pack for good measure. "Leaving no customer behind," says Martin, "that's our motto." Balancing Books and Burgers: The Superpower of a CEO Chef

Running a restaurant and acing your exams? Sounds like a recipe for disaster, right? Not for Martin. This guy's got time management skills that would make Patson Daka jealous. "Thirty minutes to an hour of studying, max," he reveals, "and boom, straight A's." He's like a culinary Usain Bolt, sprinting to the top of both the food chain and the academic ladder.

World Domination, One Chicken Wing at a Time

Martin's ambitions are as big as his appetite. Kitwe's just the appetizer, folks. The main course? Taking over the entire country, one school cafeteria at a time. University of Zambia? Mulungushi University? Consider them conquered! And if that wasn't enough, he's even got his sights set on ECL mall, planning to open a shop there in a few moon cycles (because apparently, that's how Martin measures time). This reporter's mind is officially blown.

Tonga Bull's Secret Weapon: Shawarma and BC Bliss

Ah, the Shawarma! Not just a humble wrap, but the holy grail of Tonga Bull's menu, as whispered by the man himself, Martin .

This guy could polish off five of those bad boys in a single day, no sweat. "What can I say, I have a thing for juicy, spiced-up goodness," he confesses with a grin.

But food fads aren't what really make Martin's stomach rumble. Oh no, his sales chart takes a nosedive steeper than a dropped Tonga Bul 4.

when the dreaded "BC dry spell" hits. But the moment those beautiful bursary cheques cash in, his restaurant becomes a scene straight out of a food coma mosh pit. "BC allowances? More like Tonga Bull fuel," he declares with a wink.

This tech-savvy CEO ain't messing around with pen and paper. He's got an app on his phone that tracks sales like a cheetah on Red Bull. Every sizzling shawarma, every pack of goodness , all meticulously logged in the digital domain. Talk about being ahead of the curve!

Negativity? Martin takes it like a sprinkle of extra chilli on his Shawarma – spicy, but ultimately just adding to the flavor. He's in this game for the love, the passion, the pure, unadulterated joy of serving up culinary happiness. So next time you're in Kitwe, skip the boring burger chains and dive into the wild, wonderful world of Tonga Bull. Just do yourself a favor and bring your BC bucks – the Shawarma cravings hit hard, and Martin's not one to disappoint.

The Secret to His Success: A Love of
Food and a Generous Heart

So, what's the secret ingredient that makes Tonga Bull so special? It's not just the mouthwatering meals or the mind-blowing customer service. It's Martin himself.

His passion for food is infectious, his generosity legendary, and his laugh? Well, that's enough to make even the most burnt boerewors taste like a Michelin-starred masterpiece.

So there you have it, folks. The story of the Tonga Bull, a culinary legend in the making. Remember the name, because this is just the beginning. This bull is on a mission to feed the world, one delicious dish at a time. Now, if you'll excuse me, I have a date with a plate of chips and a serious case of the Tonga Bull cravings.
